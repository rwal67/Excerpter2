About

What is Excerpter?

Excerpter is a simple program inspired by the cut-up writing technique, the practice of pulling text apart and reassembling it into novel arrangements. While the origin of this practice is as vague as its mechanics, it was popularized by Bob Dylan and William S. Burroughs (you can see a video of Bob Dylan using it below).

The meaning of such output is entirely subjective, relying on a user's sense of pattern-recognition and personal context in interpreting the result.

Pulling from 80+ original lines of text, the Excerpter generates a unique assortment of prose, peppering the text with random italicization, weight, size, and punctuation. With reckless abandon, it presents and edits an array of stock video footage to augment the reading experience.

Any and all meaning pulled from the output of this program is yours and yours alone.

Feel free to use the text here in your own writing endeavors. Who knows? This might just be the start of something special.

Bob Dylan demonstrating the cut-up technique — Don't Look Back, 1967

How was it made?

It's plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — nothing fancy under the hood. Each press pulls a fragment at random from the corpus and decides on the spot how to render it: how big, how indented, whether to italicize, whether to mess with the punctuation.

The video side runs two video elements trading off behind the scenes, so there's no black flash between clips. The longer it runs, the more it layers on top of itself — color blocks, text, strips and halves of other footage bleeding through. Everything that gets added is drawn from the same pool with equal odds, so nothing is more likely than anything else.

All video and sound components were ethically sourced. The clicking keyboard sound was sourced from freesound_community on Pixabay, and the video sources can be seen below.
